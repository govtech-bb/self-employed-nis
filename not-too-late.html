It's not too late to start

Think you're too old, or that you've left it too late? If you've worked in Barbados for any amount of time, that's almost never true. Here's what you can still do.

Your National Insurance number stays with you for life

From the first job you had to the day you retire, you keep the same National Insurance number. Your contributions from years ago still count, even if you stopped paying for a while.

If you don't know your number, NISSS can find it for you. Call 467-4NIS (4647) or visit any office with photo ID.

You can pay for up to three years you missed

If you used to pay and stopped, or you've never paid, you can pay back up to three years of contributions. NISSS adds 5% for each year you missed — so a year that ended one year ago costs an extra 5%, and a year from three years ago costs an extra 15%.

You have until 15 January each year to finish paying for the year before without any extra charge.

Close to retirement? You may still get a grant

If you don't have enough contributions to qualify for a full pension, you may qualify for a one-time grant when you reach pension age. NISSS can tell you what you'd be entitled to before you decide.

Already paying through a job? You can top up

If you have a main job and you also work for yourself on the side — selling food, doing mechanic work, hosting on Airbnb — you can pay extra contributions for your self-employed income. This gives you more cover, not less.

How to check what you've already paid

You can ask NISSS for a record of your contributions. This shows every payment from every employer over your working life. It's free, and you can collect it the same day in person.

How to ask for your record

Common worries, answered

"I had to stop at 50, I can't go back." You can. Three years of back payment is allowed, and you may still build enough years for a grant or a partial pension.

"I worked in another country, that won't count." Your time in Barbados counts on its own. Some Caribbean countries also share records with Barbados — ask NISSS about your case.

"I paid years ago but lost the receipts." NISSS keeps the records, not you. They can pull your history from your National Insurance number alone.

Source: National Insurance and Social Security Service (NISSS), May 2026. Back-payment rules are set in Regulation 4D under Act 2023-25. Grant eligibility is at the discretion of the CEO based on contribution count, not the amount paid.
